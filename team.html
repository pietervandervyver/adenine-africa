Our Team

The researchers and engineers behind Adenine Research's mission to transform hearing restoration through optical fiber cochlear implant technology.

Team Members

  • Dr. Amara Nkosi

    Founder & Principal Investigator

    Dr. Nkosi holds a PhD in Computational Neuroscience from the University of Cape Town and completed postdoctoral research at the Max Planck Institute for Neurobiology. Her work focuses on biophysical modelling of optogenetically sensitised auditory neurons and the design of light-based stimulation protocols for cochlear implants. She founded Adenine Research to bring next-generation hearing technology to patients across Africa.

    View LinkedIn Profile

  • Dr. Sipho Dlamini

    Senior Research Scientist — Photonics

    Dr. Dlamini is a photonics engineer with expertise in intracochlear optical waveguide design and fiber fabrication. He received his doctorate from Stellenbosch University and has published extensively on light delivery systems for neural interfaces. At Adenine Research he leads the development of the optical fiber arrays that form the core of the implant hardware.

    View LinkedIn Profile

  • Lerato Mokoena

    Research Engineer — Neural Signal Processing

    Lerato holds an MSc in Electrical Engineering with a specialisation in biomedical signal processing from the University of the Witwatersrand. She develops the adaptive stimulation algorithms that encode sound into optical pulse trains, and applies machine learning methods to personalise implant parameters to individual patients' auditory nerve anatomy.
