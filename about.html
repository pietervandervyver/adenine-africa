About Us

Adenine Research (Pty) Ltd. is a South African Computational Neuroscience company on a mission to transform hearing restoration through optical fiber cochlear implant technology.

Our Founding Mission

Adenine Research was founded on the conviction that the next generation of cochlear implants must be built on a foundation of rigorous Computational Neuroscience. Conventional cochlear implants rely on metal electrodes that stimulate the auditory nerve electrically — a method that has changed little in decades and carries inherent limitations in frequency resolution and long-term biocompatibility.

Our founding mission is to replace metal electrodes with optical fibers, using precisely targeted light pulses to activate individual auditory nerve fibres with far greater specificity. This approach, known as optical cochlear implantation, has the potential to restore hearing with a fidelity that current devices cannot achieve. From the outset, we have been committed to translating this science into real clinical benefit — particularly for patients across Africa, where hearing loss remains significantly underserved by existing medical technology.

Rooted in Africa

Adenine Research is headquartered in South Africa and operates from the African continent. We believe that Africa is not merely a market for imported medical technology — it is a place where world-class science can be conceived, developed, and exported to the rest of the world.

Africa carries a disproportionate burden of hearing loss, driven by factors including infectious disease, limited access to early screening, and environmental noise exposure. By situating our research and development on the continent, we ensure that the populations most in need of better hearing solutions are at the centre of our work, not an afterthought. Our African base also positions us to build partnerships with local universities, clinicians, and health systems as we move toward clinical translation.

Advancing Cochlear Implant Technology Through Computational Neuroscience

At the core of our work is Computational Neuroscience — the discipline that uses mathematical modelling, simulation, and data analysis to understand how the nervous system processes information. We apply these methods to the auditory system, building detailed biophysical models of the cochlea and auditory nerve to predict how different stimulation strategies will perform in real patients.

This computational approach allows us to design and test optical stimulation protocols in silico before any device is implanted, dramatically reducing the time and cost of iteration. It also enables us to personalise implant parameters to individual patients' neural anatomy — a level of precision that is simply not possible with conventional electrode-based devices.

Our research spans biophysical modelling of optogenetically sensitised auditory neurons, neural signal processing for sound encoding, photonic waveguide design for intracochlear light delivery, and machine learning methods for adaptive stimulation. Together, these strands of work form a coherent programme aimed at producing the next generation of cochlear implant technology.
